# Platform Naming Final Recommendation (Product + Brand Lens)

This recommendation is based on:
- current product: multi-tenant assessment SaaS, role-governed workflows, test creation, analytics
- future roadmap: adaptive learning engine, AI-assisted feedback, explainability, enterprise-grade controls
- brand need: trustworthy for institutions, modern for students, scalable beyond just "test app"

Aligned with product direction in repository references (e.g. adaptive control plane, governance, and planned **AI-assisted** capabilities such as retrieval-augmented workflows, question intelligence, and mistake-to-learning experiences).

---

## What **Evalynx.ai** means (name + `.ai` domain)

**Evalynx** (the wordmark)
- **Evaluate** — assessment, scoring, and measurable outcomes (exams, practice, analytics).
- **Lynx** — sharp perception: spotting gaps, weak topics, and patterns early (fits **adaptive** selection, diagnostics, and insight surfaces).
- Together: an **evaluation engine that “sees”** performance clearly and responds with targeted paths — not a generic “learning app” label.

**`.ai`** (the TLD)
- Signals **intelligent product positioning** without cramming “AI” into the brand spelling.
- Fits the roadmap where the platform grows **AI-assisted** features: e.g. **RAG**-style grounding for explanations and content helpers, **AI question generation** (where policy allows), smart feedback, and future adaptive/IRT-style evolution — all under **governance** (SuperAdmin → Org → test), as described in adaptive/AI planning docs.
- Practical note: `.ai` is widely understood in edtech/SaaS for “smart” platforms; pair with a primary `.com` if you want maximum enterprise email trust.

**One-line positioning you can use**
- *Evalynx.ai — sharp evaluation, intelligent learning.*

## SEO and Discoverability Rationale

Naming is not only branding. It directly impacts:
- how easily your platform is found via search
- how expensive paid search can become
- how clearly users remember and type your name

### Why **Evalynx** is strong for SEO

1. **Unique token advantage**
   - `Evalynx` is a highly distinctive word with low collision risk versus generic words like "exam", "test", or "learning".
   - This makes it easier to own the first page over time for branded queries.

2. **Brand query growth potential**
   - Once users see/hear the name, they can search exactly `Evalynx` (high intent traffic).
   - Branded traffic usually converts better than generic category traffic.

3. **Future-proof architecture**
   - The name can support SEO-friendly subpages and product clusters:
     - `Evalynx Adaptive`
     - `Evalynx Analytics`
     - `Evalynx Exam Engine`
   - This helps build topical authority without changing the core brand.

4. **Pronunciation + spelling balance**
   - Distinct but still readable in English-speaking and South Asian contexts.
   - Lower confusion risk than highly abstract names with unclear spelling.

### SEO comparison of your top 3

| Name | SEO Strength | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Evalynx** | **High** | Unique branded keyword, strong ownability, low generic competition. |
| **Cognivex** | Medium-High | Unique and premium, but less category-intuitive for early non-branded search. |
| **Examora** | Medium | Memorable, but closer to "exam" keyword space with more naming collisions. |

## Final SEO-Safe Naming Advice

If the goal is **brand power + long-term SEO efficiency**, keep:
- one strong unique master brand (**Evalynx**),
- then add descriptive product descriptors in page titles/copy (Adaptive, Exam, Analytics, AI),
- instead of forcing all keywords into the brand name itself.

This approach gives you both:
- premium positioning, and
- practical search discoverability.
